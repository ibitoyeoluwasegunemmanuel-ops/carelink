# WEEK 1 EXECUTION - TRACK C
## Parent Demand Acquisition Strategy

**Objective**: Recruit 5 beta parents ready to match with experts on Day 1 of launch

**Timeline**: 7 days parallel to Track A + Track B

**Success Metric**: 
- ✅ 50+ parents contacted
- ✅ 20+ parents interested  
- ✅ 10+ onboarding conversations
- ✅ 5+ beta users registered and onboarded

**Ownership**: Growth/Community Lead (separate from Track B expert recruitment)

**KPI**: Parent → Expert Match Count (PRIMARY METRIC)

---

## STRATEGIC APPROACH

### Why This Matters

**Without parents, experts have no one to serve.**

Supply without demand = empty marketplace = expert churn.

The math:
- 5 experts waiting → 0 parents → 0 matches → experts leave
- 5 experts + 5 parents → potential matches → proof of concept

Solution: Recruit first 5 parents in parallel with 5 experts. Create conditions for first successful match to happen in Week 1 or early Week 2.

**Success definition for this track: Parents are onboarded, informed, and READY to book the moment experts are live.**

---

## MARKET SEGMENTATION

### Target Parent Personas

**PERSONA 1: Recently Diagnosed**
- Child diagnosed with autism/ADHD in past 6 months
- Parents actively seeking "what's next"
- High urgency, high motivation
- Willing to try new platforms

Example search terms:
- "autism diagnosis Nigeria"
- "ADHD help Lagos"
- "child therapist near me"

**PERSONA 2: Frustrated with Current Options**
- Already seeing experts but unhappy (waitlists, cost, access)
- Know what they want but can't find it
- Ready to switch to better option
- Higher conversion rate

Example indicators:
- In special needs Facebook groups
- Asking "where to find therapist" on forums
- Posting about challenges

**PERSONA 3: Early Adopter**
- Tech-savvy parents
- Already using other health/wellness apps
- Willing to try new platforms
- High lifetime value

Example indicators:
- Active on tech-savvy parenting communities
- Using health tracking apps
- Following special needs tech/innovation

**PERSONA 4: NGO & School Referrals**
- Parents referred by organizations
- Pre-qualified and motivated
- Higher trust and conversion
- Organic word-of-mouth potential

Example sources:
- Special needs NGOs
- Special education schools
- Parent support groups
- Therapists recommending
